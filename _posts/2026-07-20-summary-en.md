## [Alibaba Announces Qwen 3.8 2.4T Parameter Open-Weights LLM](https://twitter.com/Alibaba_Qwen/status/2078759124914098291) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Alibaba has announced Qwen 3.8, a 2.4 trillion parameter large language model with open weights, in response to Moonshot AI's Kimi K3 announcement. This represents a significant competitive move in the AI landscape as Alibaba positions itself against other major players in the LLM space. This announcement is significant because it intensifies competition in the LLM market and makes advanced AI technology more accessible to developers and researchers through open weights. The 2.4T parameter size positions Qwen 3.8 as a major player that could influence the direction of AI development and applications. Qwen 3.8 is part of Alibaba's Qwen family of models, which are distributed under various licenses including Apache License and Qwen Research License. The model is expected to be released soon, with community members eagerly awaiting its availability on platforms like Hugging Face.

**Background**: Qwen (also known as Tongyi Qianwen) is a family of large language models developed by Alibaba Cloud. Many Qwen models are distributed under open-source licenses, making them accessible for various applications. The concept of open-weights models refers to LLMs whose trained parameters are publicly available, allowing developers to use and modify them without restriction. A 2.4 trillion parameter model represents a massive scale in AI, requiring significant computational resources for training and deployment.

## [Sam Altman Email Reveals OpenAI's Local Model Strategy](https://simonwillison.net/2026/Jul/20/sam-altman/#atom-everything) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Sam Altman's email to OpenAI's board, dated October 1, 2022 and exposed in Musk v. Altman (2026), revealed the company's strategy to create a GPT-3 level language model that can run locally on consumer hardware. This revelation is significant as it exposes OpenAI's strategic thinking regarding open source language models and their competitive approach to limit new funding for similar AI efforts by competitors like Stability AI. The email indicates OpenAI wanted to release this local model 'soon, before Stability or someone else does' to discourage others from releasing similarly-powerful models and make it harder for new efforts to get funded.

rss · Simon Willison · Jul 20, 03:47

**Background**: OpenAI is a leading artificial intelligence research laboratory known for developing advanced language models like GPT-3. GPT-3 is a powerful language model capable of generating human-like text. Stability AI is a UK-based AI company known for its open-source text-to-image model Stable Diffusion. The concept of running large language models (LLMs) locally on consumer hardware has gained attention due to improvements in model efficiency and optimization, allowing deployment on personal devices without constant internet connectivity.

This represents a significant technological achievement in planetary defense, advancing capabilities to protect Earth from potential asteroid impacts and demonstrating China's growing space technology capabilities. The test involves a projectile traveling at Mach 26 speed, which is significantly faster than conventional firearms and comparable to hypersonic vehicles like the Avangard glide vehicle.

**Background**: Planetary defense involves methods to divert or destroy near-Earth objects (NEOs) that could potentially collide with Earth. NASA successfully conducted the DART mission in 2022, which impacted the asteroid Dimorphos and changed its orbit, demonstrating the feasibility of kinetic impact deflection. China's planned mission to 2015 XF261 in 2027 is another example of international efforts to develop planetary defense capabilities.
