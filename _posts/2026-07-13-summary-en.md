## [Claude Code vs OpenCode: Token Efficiency Study Shows 33k vs 7k Tokens](https://systima.ai/blog/claude-code-vs-opencode-token-overhead) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A technical study comparing AI coding tools Claude Code and OpenCode found that Claude Code consumed 33,000 tokens while OpenCode used only 7,000 tokens for similar coding tasks. The study was conducted by adding logging between the agentic coding tools and Anthropic's endpoint to capture all requests and usage data. This finding is significant for developers and organizations concerned with AI tool costs, as it reveals substantial differences in token efficiency between popular coding assistants. The 4.7x difference in token usage could translate to major cost implications for teams using these tools extensively. The study specifically identified that Claude Code's inefficiency stems from its cache strategy and harness token usage. The researchers noted one caveat regarding their methodology and plan to update the post with more in-depth tasks, qualitative results, and reproduction of inputs and outputs.

**Background**: AI coding tools like Claude Code and OpenCode use Large Language Models (LLMs) to assist developers with coding tasks. These tools consume tokens, which are the basic units of text that LLMs process, and each token usage incurs costs. Token efficiency has become a critical consideration as organizations adopt these AI assistants, with different implementations showing varying levels of optimization in how they manage token consumption during coding tasks.

## [New sodium metal battery design charges in just 4 minutes and retains capacity for years](https://www.reddit.com/r/technology/comments/1uufxgh/new_sodium_metal_battery_design_charges_in_just_4/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Chinese researchers have developed a new sodium metal battery design that can charge from zero to full in just 4 minutes while maintaining its capacity for years, according to findings reported in the journal Nano-Micro Letters. This breakthrough could revolutionize energy storage technology, potentially impacting electric vehicles and renewable energy systems by providing a faster-charging, long-lasting alternative to current lithium-ion batteries. The new battery design addresses key challenges in sodium metal batteries including dendrite growth and unstable solid electrolyte interphase (SEI) layers, achieving ultra-fast charging capabilities and 6,000-hour stability as reported by researchers.

reddit · r/technology · /u/lurker_bee · Jul 12, 13:45

**Background**: Sodium-ion batteries (NIBs) are rechargeable batteries that use sodium ions as charge carriers, similar to lithium-ion batteries but with sodium replacing lithium. They offer potential cost advantages and are being explored as alternatives to lithium-ion technology, especially for large-scale energy storage applications. Sodium metal batteries (SMBs) specifically aim for higher energy density but face challenges like dendrite formation and stability issues that this new design appears to address.
